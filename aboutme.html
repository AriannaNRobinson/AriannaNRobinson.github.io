My Story...

I’ve always had a long list of interests, so it was challenging at first to find my true passion.

My Past...

When I was in grade school and middle school, I swore that I would become an author or an artist. I had a very creative mind, large imagination, and loved writing short stories and drawing characters for those stories in my free time. As I grew older, I grew to love math, science, and solving complex equations. I loved the reward that came with solving complex problems, and completed all of the courses in those subjects that my highschool offered. When I started college, I started pursuing a biomedical sciences major, but soon realized I wanted to switch into engineering, after seeing how my engineering friends could combine their creativity, and problem solving skills, and use technology to create something amazing. However, at that time I wasn’t able to switch into the strictly structured engineering program because my scholarship was only for four years. I decided to pursue other passions, and received a double Bachelor’s degree in economics and political science. I struggled in the job market, landing on a customer service job. Due to my competitive drive, within a few months, I designed and implemented a training program for new employees, received an award for top sales contribution, and was promoted to lead a specialized department where I wore many hats. Unfortunately, I was not happy, and kept wondering what would have happened if I went into the tech field all those years ago.

The Plan...

After a lot of research, I decided to save as much money as I could, make a budget plan, and risk everything by quitting my job and pursuing a career in software engineering through the App Academy bootcamp. It was the most challenging and rewarding experience. After many long days of coding all day and studying all night, I made it through, and I couldn’t be happier. Designing websites from the back-end to the front-end, felt like it took my interests, my creativity, my love for problem solving, and my competitive nature, and put them all together into one singular passion. Through my time at App Academy, I was in class all day, Mondays through Friday, studied all evening, and completed assessments almost every Monday. I learned and applied many languages, frameworks, and concepts to create four full stack projects. I especially enjoyed implementing React and Redux into my projects and watching my applications come to life.

The Conclusion...

Because I discovered my true passion a bit late, it gave me the opportunity to dive into quite a few different fields of knowledge. I believe that I have an extremely diverse set of skills from these experiences. My creativity and my imagination started early in my childhood. I learned compassion, cooperation, and teamwork through customer service, as well as patience when training new employees. I learned about the importance of deadlines and accuracy through sales. I learned important leadership, planning, and organization skills from management. I explored the rewarding feeling of solving complex problems through my explorations with math and science classes. I learned about structure and how to solve real world issues through studying economics and political science. Combining all of those experiences, not only make me a great software developer, but they will make me a great fit for any situation I find myself in.
